## Context

The client was selling electronics through marketplaces and taking a ~15% platform fee on every transaction. They wanted to migrate to a self-hosted store with full control over checkout, inventory, and customer data. The hard constraint: it had to ship in under 8 weeks.

## Decisions

- **Next.js App Router** for SSR on product pages — SEO matters for an electronics store competing on long-tail product searches.
- **PostgreSQL over MongoDB** — relational data (products, orders, customers, line items) maps cleanly to SQL and queries stay fast as inventory grows.
- **Custom admin dashboard** instead of off-the-shelf (Strapi/Sanity) — the client's workflow had quirks (B2B pricing tiers) that didn't fit standard CMS shapes.
- **Stripe for payments**, not a custom gateway integration — boring choice, but it earned its place.

## Tradeoffs

Skipped a proper search index (Algolia/Meilisearch) for v1 and used Postgres full-text search. It's slower at scale but kept the infra footprint to one database. A migration path exists when product count crosses ~10k.

Cart state lives in `localStorage` + server reconciliation rather than a full server-side cart with sessions. Simpler, but means cross-device cart sync is a future feature.

## Outcome

Shipped in 7 weeks. The client cut transaction fees by ~12% on day one and gained direct customer email access for marketing. Order volume in month two was within 8% of their marketplace baseline despite the migration friction.
