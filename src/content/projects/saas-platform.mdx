## The situation

Every SaaS project starts with the same checklist: sign-up, login, password resets, team invitations, billing, role-based permissions. After building this three times for three different products, I decided to build it once, properly, and reuse it.

## What I decided

- **Email magic links instead of passwords.** Passwords are a security headache. Magic links shift the responsibility back to the email provider, who's far better at securing accounts than I am.
- **Stripe's own billing portal** instead of building my own. Less code to maintain, less to break, and customers already trust Stripe's UI.
- **Workspaces as first-class citizens.** Every piece of data belongs to a workspace, automatically. No accidentally showing one customer another's information.
- **Server-side rendering by default**, with interactive parts only where they earn their place.

## What I traded off

The workspace separation is enforced by application code, not by the database itself. That's faster to build and good enough for the scale I'm targeting. If a project grows, there's a documented path to a stronger isolation model.

Magic links are great for security but worse for users on email systems that aggressively filter automated mail (looking at you, corporate Outlook). I added Google and GitHub sign-in as a fallback.

## How it turned out

This foundation is now powering two production SaaS products. The time it takes me to get a new SaaS idea from "blank repo" to "working prototype" dropped from about three weeks to about three days. Worth every hour I spent building it.
