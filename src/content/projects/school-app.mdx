## The situation

A school with 600 students was drowning in admin. Attendance was being taken on paper, copied into spreadsheets, then lost. Grades lived in one teacher's laptop. Parent updates went out via half a dozen WhatsApp groups. The headmistress wanted one tool that handled all of it — and the deal was that teachers had to be productive on it from day one. Day thirty was too late.

## What I decided

- **Mobile-first, no exceptions.** Teachers take attendance on their phones, walking between classrooms. Desktop is the secondary form factor.
- **Mark and forget.** When a teacher taps a student's name, the green dot appears immediately, even if the wifi is flaky. The data syncs in the background. This single decision saved more frustration than anything else.
- **Different home screens for different people.** Teachers, the admin office, and parents each see a screen built for their day. No menus to dig through.
- **Server-generated PDFs for report cards.** The output had to match the school's letterhead exactly. We built a small pipeline that produces the same file, every time.

## What I traded off

No fancy grading-rubric builder in v1. Teachers enter raw scores, and the system converts them into letter grades against a simple cutoff table the school can edit. If they want a proper rubric editor, we'll build it in v2.

No mobile app — just a responsive website. Notifications go via SMS and email. It's cheaper, it works on every phone, and there's no app store approval to wait for.

## How it turned out

Every teacher is using it daily. Time spent on attendance and reports dropped from about six hours a week to under one. Parent engagement — measured by message read rates — tripled compared to the WhatsApp baseline. Most importantly, the headmistress can finally see real numbers across the school instead of asking each teacher individually.
