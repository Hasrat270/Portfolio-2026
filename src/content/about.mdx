## Hi, I'm Hasrat

I build websites and software for people. That's the short version.

The longer version: I work with founders and small teams to turn ideas — the kind that start on the back of a napkin — into actual, working products. Online stores, dashboards, internal tools, the occasional weekend experiment that turns into something real.

## What that looks like in practice

Most of my work falls into one of three buckets:

- **Custom web apps.** Things that need to talk to a database, handle payments, manage users, send emails — the stuff a website builder can't quite do.
- **Admin dashboards and internal tools.** The screens your team actually uses every day. They should feel calm, not chaotic.
- **End-to-end product builds.** Sometimes a founder has an idea and needs someone to take it from rough notes to a live URL. I'm comfortable being that person.

## How I think about work

A few things I've learned the hard way:

- **Small and finished beats big and almost-done.** Ship the smallest useful version, then improve it. Always.
- **Plain words win.** If I can't explain what a feature does in one sentence, the feature probably isn't ready.
- **Boring tools, mostly.** I reach for proven, well-documented technology by default. Novelty has to earn its place.
- **The next person matters.** I try to write code and notes that someone else (often future-me) can pick up without cursing.
